Introductory Read Aloud

A member of a heretical Dwarven sect of the Cult of the Dragon disguises herself as a street urchin and implores the PARTY to rid her clan's (Gorunn) mountaintop tomb of some evil. She hopes that by restoring the sanctity of the tomb, her ancestors will forgive her of some transgression that caused her to be expelled from the clan.

This adventure takes place on an unassuming mountaintop in the Dragonspine Mountains.

Chamber 1

Entrance Room

Found?

Read Aloud:

As the wind whips through the peaks around you, the strongest members of your party struggle against two towering iron doors in an attempt to find respite from the weather. Redarc manages to pry open a gap just large enough for the rest of the party to slip past, then makes it into a large circular entrance hall just as the wind slams the door shut behind him. To your surprise you see a blinding mote of light shoot up the 30 foot seam between the doors, leaving a trail of hot metal, and welding them shut.

As you take stock of your new surroundings, you find yourself in a circular room, 40 feet in diameter, the most notable feature of which is a stone well in the center of the room, surrounded by a grated floor. You are obviously not the first creatures to take shelter here, as there is camping gear from at least a dozen cultures cramped around the outer perimeter of the chamber. Also stacked in a few piles around the room are Dwarven implements for carving through this vast mass of granite.

The air is slightly humid, with a moldy stench, that seems to carry an otherwordly Dwarven chanting from all directions.

Dominating the southeastern wall is a large tapestry of the Dwarven god of creation, Moradin, however it appears to have been defaced by someone who cut out the eyes and hands. Two braziers producing magical white fire sit on each side of the tapestry and illuminate the room. Below the tapestry is a rug with various offerings piled on it.

To the North, East, and South, you see 10-foot wide passages heading deeper into the tomb...


Mechanics:

Detailed Search:

Among the offerings, you find nothing of particular value: an offertory container with a hammer and anvil symbol, a bag containing some dust, a basket with some cotton, and a plain tin platter holding a broken skull. Sifting through the camping gear, you find an old contract for the discovery of antiquities, a book on armor making, a beard brush, and a travelogue for the land of Laerakond.

Perception DC 18:

You also notice that the grate around the well seems to be stained with blood.

Chamber 2

Pristine Burial Chamber

Found?

Read-Aloud:

As you enter this room, you are surprised to see that it seems to have remained untouched by the blight that has spread through the rest of this tomb.

The room is octagonal in shape, about 40 ft. wide, with two large sarcophagi at the west end, and two ranks of pews facing them. A single brazier hung from the center of the ceiling provides dim light for the entire room, and a lit incense burner near the entrance indicates a slight draft blowing towards you, but the air in here is otherwise clear and still.

The only exit from this chamber is to the East, through the passage behind you.


Mechanics:

Detailed Search:

Searching around the room yields some of the normal accoutrements associated with a holy place: a robe, some idols, more incense burners, and interestingly enough, a quill and last testament underneath some cobwebs. The ink on the testament has faded too far to determine the testator or the executor.

Dwarvish Language Comprehension:

The inscriptions on the sarcophagi indicate that this is the resting place of the esteemed ancestors of the Gorunn clan, Baern and Helja.

This unlit 20 ft. by 20 ft. square room looks as though it has endured a formidable fire. There are several small mounds of ash scattered around the room and you can make out the remnants of burned textiles.

The only exit is to the East through the passage you entered from.
